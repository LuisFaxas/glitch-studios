### Human Verification Required

#### 1. Nav Item Count (375px viewport)

**Test:** Open site on a 375px viewport, tap Menu in the bottom tab bar.
**Expected:** Only 3 nav tiles visible — Book Session, Artists, Contact. No Beats, Services, Portfolio, Blog tiles. No Now Playing widget.
**Why human:** Code is correct (`mobileMenuItems` filters to exactly 3 items). Device rendering confirms no regressions in the consuming component.

#### 2. Viewport Fit — No Scrolling

**Test:** With menu open at 375px viewport, attempt to scroll the overlay. Verify no cutoff.
**Expected:** All 4 rows (logo, nav, widgets, auth+close) fit within the visible screen without scrolling. No content clipped by safe-area insets.
**Why human:** `overflow-hidden` and `100dvh` are set, but iOS safe-area inset math and actual tile heights on a real device must be confirmed visually.

#### 3. Stagger Animation Quality

**Test:** Open menu and watch the entrance animation play.
**Expected:** Logo tile enters first (slides down from above), then nav row, widgets row, auth row each slide up at ~80ms intervals. Total animation feels fluid and fast, completing within ~420ms.
**Why human:** Timing constants are correct in code, but felt quality and visual smoothness require eyes-on review.

#### 4. Swipe-Down Dismiss Gesture

**Test:** With menu open, slowly swipe down and release before the threshold. Then swipe down quickly past the threshold.
**Expected:** Slow release snaps back to full position. Quick downward swipe with velocity dismisses the menu. Overlay fades progressively during drag.
**Why human:** Touch gesture requires Chrome DevTools touch simulation or real mobile device.

#### 5. Reduce Motion Bypass

**Test:** Enable "Reduce Motion" in system accessibility settings. Open and close menu.
**Expected:** Menu appears and disappears instantly. No slide animation, no stagger delays.
**Why human:** Requires OS-level accessibility setting activation.

#### 6. Cyberpunk Metro Aesthetic Match

**Test:** Compare mobile menu overlay to desktop sidebar/tile nav visually.
**Expected:** Consistent tile styling, mono fonts, dark backgrounds, sharp borders. Feels like a cohesive extension of the desktop design language.
**Why human:** Aesthetic quality is subjective and requires visual judgment.
